# Source Corpora and Example Provenance

## Status

All example utterances in the current MVP are `constructed_minimal_pair`:
created for controlled experimental contrast between pragmatic variants.
No examples are drawn from real customer conversations or published corpora.

## Provenance labels used in this project

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `constructed_minimal_pair` | Created by the authors to isolate a specific pragmatic device. Not corpus-attested. |
| `corpus_attested` | Drawn from a published corpus or dataset, with source cited. |

## Current example inventory

All examples in `docs/minimal_pair_examples.md` are `constructed_minimal_pair`.

## Planned corpus sources (for future iterations)

If corpus-attested examples are added, they will be drawn from:
- CCSARP (Blum-Kulka et al. 1989) for cross-linguistic request/refusal patterns
- Annotated service dialogue datasets if available under research license

## Limitation note

The use of constructed examples is a deliberate methodological choice for
controlled minimal-pair design. It is not a claim about naturalistic customer
language. Corpus validation is a planned future step.
